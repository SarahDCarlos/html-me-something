A Brief History of Matcha

The tale of matcha dates back to the Tang Dynasty in China, around the 7th to 10th centuries. the method of making powdered tea from steam-prepared dried tea leaves, became popular in 12th century. That is when matcha was discovered by a Buddhist monk, Myoan Eisai, and brought to Japan. Drinking matcha not only aided the meditation efforts of Zen monks but also became a means in attaining enlightenment itself.

Sacrificing a bowl of the drink to Buddha was part of everyday temple practices. the ensuing Song Dynasty, which reigned from the 10th â€“ 13th, is largely credited with making this form of tea preparation popular. Eisai, a Japanese Buddhist Monk, spent the better part of his life studying Buddhism in China. In 1191, Eisai returned permanently to Japan, bringing with him tea seeds along with the Zen Buddhist methods of preparing powdered green tea.

My favorite Way To Make Matcha

As a fusion!

One of my favorite drinks to cool off in the summer has always been an Aqua Fresca. An Aqua Fresca is a light Mexican beverage that is made using a blend of fruits, seeds, grains, or flowers. By far, my favorite aqua fresha is an horchata, it is a sweet rice and cinnamon version of an aqua fresca. When I am needing a pick me up especially in the morning, I add Matcha to the mix to make it a super light Latte.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup Matcha Powder
  • 1 cup Rice
  • 2 liters room temperature water
  • 4 oz hot water
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1 liter milk

The night before you decide to make this recipe, you need to soak the rice in a bowl of water to soften it.

Place water and rice into a blender and blend together until fully liquified.

Using a cheese cloth, place the cloth in the pitcher you plant to use and pour the rice into the cheese cloth. Squeeze the liquid out of the cheese cloth.

Add in sugar and milk and stir until fully dissolved

In a seperate cup mix hot water and matcha powder, make sure to stir well until it fully dissolves.

Add the matcha in with the pitcher of rice water.

Add ice and enjoy!
